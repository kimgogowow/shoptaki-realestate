Cap rate is expressed as a percentage and represents the rate of return an investor can expect to receive on their investment in the property. The higher the cap rate, the higher the potential return, but it also indicates a higher level of risk. Conversely, a lower cap rate indicates a lower potential return, but also a lower level of risk. Cap rate is often used as a benchmark to compare the value of different real estate investment opportunities.
The loan-to-value (LTV) ratio is an assessment of lending risk that financial institutions and other lenders examine before approving a mortgage. Typically, loan assessments with high LTV ratios are considered higher-risk loans. Therefore, if the mortgage is approved, the loan has a higher interest rate.
